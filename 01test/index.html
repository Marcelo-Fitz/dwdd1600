Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship refers to the responsible and ethical use of technology, including understanding how to use digital tools safely, respectfully, and effectively in both online and offline environments.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the legal framework that governs the use of digital technologies and online activities, including issues like privacy, intellectual property, and cybersecurity.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a set of rules that define how users are allowed to use an organization's digital resources, such as computers, networks, and software.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work or ideas without giving proper credit.

Often by copying text, images, or other content and presenting it as one's own.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty refers to any act of cheating or plagiarism in an academic setting, such as copying from another student, using unauthorized materials during an exam, or submitting work that is not your own.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

When using AI-generated content, it is important to cite the source, ensure the content is accurate and appropriate, and avoid submitting AI-generated work as your own original work.
